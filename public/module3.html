Prospecting · Lesson 1

Finding Businesses That Need You Right Now

You do not need to cold call strangers or spend money on ads. The best clients are businesses you can find in 10 minutes online — businesses that are already showing signs they need what you offer.

The Right Mindset — You Are Solving a Real Problem
Before you start looking for clients, understand this: you are not a salesperson trying to convince someone to buy something they don't need. You are a problem solver approaching businesses that are already losing time and money every day because they don't have what you build.
When a restaurant owner gets 30 WhatsApp messages per day and can only reply to 10 of them, they are losing clients right now. You have the solution. Your job is simply to show them that the solution exists and that you can build it for them.
Method 1 — Google Maps (Best for Beginners)
Google Maps shows you every local business in any area. It is the fastest way to build a list of prospects because you can see their contact information, their reviews, and how active they are.
1
Open Google Maps on your phone or computer
Go to maps.google.com or open the Google Maps app on your phone. Make sure you are logged into your Google account.
2
Search for your chosen industry in your city
In the search bar at the top, type your niche and city. Examples:
restaurants Bucharest
dental clinic Cluj
real estate agency Timisoara
beauty salon Iasi

Press Enter. The map shows all matching businesses with red pins. On the left side you see a list of businesses with their names, star ratings, and number of reviews.
3
Filter for the best prospects
Look for businesses with these characteristics — they are the most likely to pay for your automation:
✓ 50+ reviews — means they have enough customers to justify automation. A business with 5 reviews is too small.

✓ 3.5–4.5 star rating — good enough to be credible but not perfect. Perfect businesses often don't feel urgency to improve.

✓ Has a phone number listed — means they are contactable. Click on the business to see if they have a WhatsApp number.

✓ Has a website or social media listed — means they care about their online presence.

✗ Skip businesses with only 1–2 reviews — too small to invest in automation.
✗ Skip businesses that say "Permanently closed".
4
Click on each business to get their contact details
Click on a business in the list. A panel opens on the left showing: their name, address, phone number, website, hours, and sometimes a WhatsApp button. Write down or screenshot:
— Business name
— Phone number (this is often their WhatsApp number too)
— Website URL (you will visit it to understand their business better)
— What type of business they are exactly

Do this for 10–15 businesses. This is your prospect list.
5
Visit their website or Instagram to understand their pain
Before contacting any business, spend 3 minutes on their website or Instagram. Ask yourself: Do they have a contact form? Do they list a WhatsApp number? Do they reply quickly to Instagram comments? This research helps you write a personalized message in Lesson 2.
The best signal: look at their Instagram or Facebook page. If they post regularly but have comments with questions like "What are your hours?" or "Do you take bookings?" with no replies — that business is losing clients every day because they cannot respond fast enough. That is your opportunity.
Method 2 — Instagram (Best for Coaches & Online Businesses)
1
Open Instagram and use the search feature
Open Instagram on your phone. Tap the search icon (magnifying glass) at the bottom. In the search bar type a hashtag related to your niche. Examples:
#restaurantbucharest
#coachonline
#fitnesstrainer
#realestateagent

Tap the hashtag. You see posts from businesses using that tag. Tap on posts that look like business accounts (not personal accounts).
2
Look for the golden signal — unanswered DMs
On a business's Instagram profile, look at their recent posts. Tap on the comments. Look for questions from followers that the business has NOT replied to — things like "What is the price?", "Do you ship to X?", "How do I book?", "Are you open on Sunday?"
If you see 5+ unanswered questions in their comments — that business is losing potential clients every day. They desperately need an automated reply system. This is your perfect prospect. Add them to your list.
3
Find their contact information
On their Instagram profile page, look for:
— Email button (tap it to see their business email)
— Phone/WhatsApp button (tap to see their number)
— Link in bio (often leads to their website where you can find more contact info)
— Direct message them on Instagram — many business owners check DMs regularly

Add their contact details to your prospect list.
Method 3 — LinkedIn (Best for B2B and Agencies)
1
Go to linkedin.com and use the search
Open linkedin.com. In the search bar at the top, type a job title and location. Examples: CEO restaurant Bucharest or real estate agent Cluj or clinic owner Romania.
2
Filter results to "People"
After searching, click the People filter tab at the top of the results. This shows individual people — business owners, managers, directors — not company pages.
3
Look at their profile and connect
Click on profiles that match your target. Look at their company size (in the "Experience" section) — aim for companies with 5–50 employees. Larger companies are harder to close, smaller ones may not have the budget. Click Connect and add a personalized note.
LinkedIn connection note example:
"Hi [Name], I help restaurants in Romania automate their customer replies using AI — saving 10+ hours per week. Would love to connect and share how it works."
✦ The Golden Signal — Businesses Hiring Virtual Assistants
Go to LinkedIn Jobs or Indeed.ro and search for "virtual assistant," "customer service," or "social media manager" in your city. Every company posting one of these jobs is paying $500–$1,500/month for a person to do work that your AI automation does for $150–$400/month. They already know they have a problem — you just offer a better solution.
Key Takeaways — Lesson 1
Google Maps is the fastest way to find local business prospects — 10 businesses in 10 minutes.
Unanswered Instagram comments = businesses actively losing clients. These are your best prospects.
Businesses hiring virtual assistants are pre-qualified prospects — they already know they need help with repetitive tasks.
Build a list of 10–15 prospects before writing a single message. Quality over quantity at this stage.
✦ Action — Build Your Prospect List Now
Open Google Maps right now. Search for your chosen industry in your city. Find 10 businesses that match the criteria above. Write down their name, phone number, Instagram handle (if they have one), and one sentence about what repetitive problem they likely have. This is your first prospect list. Do not contact anyone yet — that comes in Lesson 2.
Outreach · Lesson 2

The Outreach Message That Gets Replies

Most outreach fails because it talks about you. The message that works talks about what they are losing — time, leads, money. This lesson gives you exact copy-paste messages for WhatsApp, Instagram DM, and email.

Why Most Outreach Gets Ignored
Business owners receive dozens of sales messages every week. Most of them say the same thing: "Hi, I offer digital marketing services, can we hop on a call?" These get ignored because they are generic, talk about the sender, and give no reason for the business owner to care.
The message that gets a reply is specific to their business, identifies a real problem they have right now, and shows you already understand their situation — before they have told you anything.
The Formula — Hook, Pain, Proof, Ask
Every effective outreach message has four parts:
H
Hook — A specific observation about their business
Start with something specific about THEM — not about you. Reference something you noticed on their Google Maps, Instagram, or website. This immediately shows you are not sending a generic mass message.
Example: "I noticed you have 280 Google reviews which is impressive for a restaurant your size" or "I saw on your Instagram that you get a lot of comments asking about bookings."
P
Pain — Name the exact problem and its consequence
Describe the specific problem they have and what it costs them. Be concrete — mention time lost, leads lost, or money lost. People act when they feel the pain of a problem, not when they hear about abstract solutions.
Example: "That probably means you're getting 20–30 messages per day on WhatsApp and Instagram that you can't reply to fast enough — which means losing clients to competitors who reply instantly."
S
Solution — What you do in one sentence
Describe your solution in the simplest possible terms. No technical jargon. Focus on the outcome they get, not the technology you use.
Example: "I build AI systems that reply to every WhatsApp and Instagram message instantly — 24/7, even at 3am, with responses that sound exactly like your team."
A
Ask — Request a 15-minute call, not a sale
Never ask for a sale in the first message. Ask for a short call. A 15-minute call feels low commitment and easy to say yes to. You close the sale on the call — not in the message.
Example: "Would a 15-minute call this week make sense to show you exactly how it would work for your restaurant?"
Copy-Paste Template 1 — WhatsApp Message (Restaurants)
This is a complete WhatsApp message you can send to restaurant owners. Replace the parts in [brackets] with real details from your research:
WhatsApp — Restaurant
Hi [Owner Name], I noticed [Restaurant Name] has [X] Google reviews — impressive for a restaurant in [City]. I'm guessing you and your team get a lot of WhatsApp and Instagram messages every day asking about your menu, hours, bookings, and prices — and it's probably impossible to reply to all of them quickly. I build AI systems that automatically reply to every message within seconds — 24/7, with responses that match your restaurant's style. Your team never has to answer the same question twice. A restaurant similar to yours in [City] implemented this and stopped losing bookings to unanswered messages. Would a 15-minute call this week make sense to show you exactly how it works?
Keep it as is. Do not add more. Do not make it longer. Short messages get more replies than long ones.
Copy-Paste Template 2 — Instagram DM (Any Business)
Instagram DM — Universal
Hi [Name] 👋 I was looking at your profile and noticed you get quite a few comments and DMs asking questions — but a lot of them seem to go unanswered. That's probably costing you leads every day — people who message and don't hear back just go to a competitor. I build AI systems that reply instantly to every DM and comment automatically — 24/7, in your brand voice. Would you be open to a quick 15-min call to see if it could work for you?
Send this as a DM. If they have their DMs restricted, use the email from their bio instead.
Copy-Paste Template 3 — Email Outreach (Professional)
Email — Subject: Quick question about [Business Name]
Hi [Name], I came across [Business Name] on Google and was impressed by your [X] reviews. Quick question — how are you currently handling customer inquiries that come in outside business hours? WhatsApp messages, Instagram DMs, contact form submissions? The reason I ask: most businesses in your industry lose 20–40% of potential clients simply because they can't reply fast enough. I build AI automation systems that reply instantly to every inquiry — 24/7, personalized to your business — for a fraction of the cost of hiring someone. Would it be worth a 15-minute call this week to show you exactly how it works for businesses like yours? [Your Name]
Subject line: "Quick question about [Business Name]" — this subject line gets opened because it is personal and curiosity-inducing.
How to Send — Platform by Platform
1
Sending via WhatsApp
Open WhatsApp. Tap the new chat icon. Enter the business phone number you found on Google Maps. Write or paste your message. Before sending, personalize the [brackets] with their real info. Read it once to check for mistakes. Send.
If the number is not on WhatsApp, it will say "This phone number is not registered on WhatsApp." In that case, call them directly or find their email and use Template 3.
2
Sending via Instagram DM
Go to their Instagram profile. Tap the Message button under their bio. The DM window opens. Paste Template 2 with their real name filled in. Tap Send.
Instagram sometimes restricts DMs from accounts that don't follow each other. If your DM request gets stuck in their "Message Requests" folder, it may take longer for them to see it. Follow their account before sending to increase visibility.
3
Sending via Email
Open your Gmail. Click Compose. In the "To" field enter their email address. In the "Subject" field type: Quick question about [Business Name]. Paste Template 3 in the body with their details filled in. Read it once. Click Send.
4
Follow up if no reply after 3 days
If they don't reply in 3 days, send one follow-up message. Keep it short:
"Hi [Name], just following up on my message from [day]. Totally understand if timing isn't right — just wanted to make sure it didn't get lost. Happy to share a quick example of what I've built for similar businesses if that would help."

Send maximum 2 messages total — one initial and one follow-up. If they don't reply after 2 messages, move on to the next prospect. Never spam.
How Many Messages to Send Per Day
Send 5–10 personalized messages per day. This takes about 30–45 minutes if you have done your research from Lesson 1. After 2 weeks of consistent outreach you will have sent 70–140 messages. At a 10–15% reply rate that is 7–21 conversations. From those conversations you typically close 1–3 clients.
The key word is personalized. Do not copy-paste the same message to 100 businesses without changing anything. Personalize the hook — the first line — for every single business. That is what separates a 2% reply rate from a 15% reply rate.
⚠️ What Not to Do
Do not lead with price. Do not say "I offer cheap services." Do not send a long paragraph about your skills and experience. Do not use the word "freelancer" — say "I build AI systems" or "I run an automation agency." Do not ask for a long meeting — always ask for 15 minutes. Do not send more than 2 follow-ups to the same person.
Key Takeaways — Lesson 2
Every message must start with something specific about their business — not about you.
Always ask for a 15-minute call — never try to sell in the first message.
Send 5–10 personalized messages per day. Consistency beats volume.
Follow up once after 3 days. If no reply, move on. Never send more than 2 messages total.
✦ Action — Send Your First 5 Messages Today
Take the prospect list you built in Lesson 1. Choose the 5 businesses you feel most confident about. Personalize Template 1 or 2 for each one — change the hook so it references something specific about that business. Send all 5 today. Write down who you contacted and when. This is the beginning of your pipeline.
Sales · Lesson 3

Closing the Deal & Setting Your Price

Someone replied and wants to talk. Now you have a 15-minute call to understand their problem and close the deal. This lesson gives you the exact script — what to say, when to say it, and how to handle every common objection.

Before the Call — How to Prepare in 5 Minutes
1
Research their business for 5 minutes
Before the call, spend 5 minutes reviewing their website, Instagram, and Google Maps listing. Make notes of: how many reviews they have, what their main service is, whether they list a contact form or WhatsApp, and what problems their reviews mention.
The goal is to walk into the call already knowing their business so you can ask smart questions and give relevant examples. Nothing impresses a business owner more than someone who has done their homework.
2
Prepare your screen share or demo
If you have already built the lead follow-up automation from Module 2, be ready to show it. Open Make.com and have your scenario visible so you can share your screen during the call and show it actually working.
If you have not built anything yet — build the Module 2 automation before your first call. Even a working demo with test data is 100x more convincing than a verbal description.
The Call Script — Minute by Minute
0–2
Opening — Build rapport quickly
Start the call warmly and get to the point fast. Business owners respect people who value their time.
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy so I'll keep this to 15 minutes as I promised. Just to confirm — you're the owner of [Business Name], right? Great. I had a look at your [website/Instagram] before the call and I noticed [specific thing]. That's actually why I reached out."
2–7
Discovery — Ask these exact questions
Your only job in this part of the call is to ask questions and listen. Do not talk about yourself or your service yet. Ask:
Question 1: "How do customers usually contact you when they want to inquire about [your service/product]?"

Question 2: "How long does it usually take your team to reply to a WhatsApp or Instagram message?"

Question 3: "Have you ever had a potential client go to a competitor just because you couldn't reply fast enough?"

Question 4 (the money question): "Roughly how much is a new client worth to your business over their lifetime?"

Listen carefully. Their answers tell you exactly what to focus on when you present your solution.
7–12
Presentation — Show the solution
Now present your solution based on what they told you. Do not give a generic pitch — reference exactly what they said:
"So based on what you told me — you get about [X] messages per day and it takes [Y] hours to reply — here's what I can build for you: an AI system that replies to every WhatsApp message within seconds, 24 hours a day. The reply sounds exactly like your team — I customize it with your menu, your prices, your style. No one knows it's automated."

Then show your demo if you have one — share your screen and show the Make.com scenario and a sample email it generated. Even 60 seconds of a live demo is worth more than 10 minutes of talking.
12–15
Pricing and Close
After the presentation, quote your price confidently. Do not apologize for your price and do not lower it immediately if they hesitate.
"To build and set this up for your business it's [€400–€800] one time. After that it's [€150–€250] per month for me to maintain it, update it, and make sure it keeps working perfectly. The setup takes about a week."

Then stop talking. Wait for their response. Silence is normal — let them think.
How to Handle the Most Common Objections
1
"It's too expensive"
Do not lower your price immediately. Instead, reframe the value:
"I understand. Let me ask — you said a new client is worth about [€X] to you. If this system helps you capture just 2 extra clients per month that you would have otherwise lost, it pays for itself in the first month. After that it's pure profit. Does that make sense?"

If they still hesitate after this, you can offer to lower the setup fee for your first client in exchange for a testimonial: "Since you'd be one of my first clients in this industry, I can do the setup for €200 instead of €600 — in exchange for a written review of the results after 30 days."
2
"Let me think about it"
This usually means they are interested but have a specific concern they haven't voiced. Ask:
"Of course, take your time. Can I ask — is there a specific concern you want to think through? I want to make sure I've answered all your questions before we end the call."

This question often surfaces the real objection — price, trust, technical fear — which you can then address directly.
3
"I'm not technical, I don't understand how this works"
Reassure them that they don't need to understand the technology:
"You don't need to understand any of the technical side — that's my job. You just tell me what you want the system to say and I set everything up. From your side, it's completely invisible. Messages come in, professional replies go out, and you never have to touch it."
4
"Can I see it working first before I pay?"
This is a great sign — they are interested but want proof. Offer a mini demo:
"Absolutely. Give me your WhatsApp number and I'll send you a test message right now so you can see exactly what your customers would receive."

Then send them a message from your test automation and let them see the AI reply arrive in seconds. This closes more deals than any amount of talking.
Your Pricing Structure
Use this pricing as your starting point. You can adjust based on the complexity of what they need and how much they told you they can spend:
$
Simple automation — WhatsApp or Instagram bot only
Setup fee: $300–$600 one time. Monthly retainer: $100–$200/month. Build time: 2–4 hours.
$$
Full lead management system — form + email + CRM
Setup fee: $600–$1,200 one time. Monthly retainer: $200–$350/month. Build time: 4–8 hours.
$$$
Complete system — WhatsApp + Instagram + email + CRM + reports
Setup fee: $1,200–$2,000 one time. Monthly retainer: $300–$500/month. Build time: 8–16 hours.
✦ Your First Client Strategy
For your very first client, consider offering the setup for free or at a very low cost ($50–$100) in exchange for a monthly retainer and a written testimonial after 30 days. One real case study — "Restaurant X got 3 extra bookings in the first week after implementing the system" — is worth more than any amount of cold outreach. It becomes your most powerful sales tool for every client after.
Key Takeaways — Lesson 3
Spend the first 5 minutes of the call asking questions — not talking about yourself. Their answers tell you exactly how to close.
A live demo — even 60 seconds — closes more deals than 10 minutes of talking. Build the Module 2 automation before your first call.
When they say "it's too expensive" — reframe with value. Calculate how many clients the system would need to recover to pay for itself.
Your first client can be at a reduced price in exchange for a testimonial. That testimonial becomes your most powerful sales asset.

Send your outreach messages from Lesson 2. When someone replies and shows interest, respond immediately and suggest two specific times for a 15-minute call. Example: "Great! Would Tuesday at 10am or Wednesday at 3pm work for a quick 15-minute call? I can show you exactly how it would work for [their business name]." Book the call. Practice the script from this lesson out loud before you get on the call. You know more than you think you do.
