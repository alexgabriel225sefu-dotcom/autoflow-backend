Make.com Setup — Your Automation Hub

Make.com is the platform where you build all your automations visually — no code needed. In this lesson you create your free account and learn every part of the interface so you feel completely confident before building.

What is Make.com and Why We Use It
Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform. You connect apps together by dragging circles and drawing lines between them. Each circle is an app — Google Sheets, Gmail, OpenAI, WhatsApp — and the lines represent data flowing between them automatically.
Why Make.com and not Zapier? Make.com gives you more flexibility, more control, and 1,000 free operations per month. For your first 2–3 clients the free plan is more than enough. You never need to write a single line of code.
Step 1 — Create Your Free Account
1
Open your browser and go to make.com
Open Google Chrome, Safari, or any browser on your phone or computer. In the address bar at the top, type make.com and press Enter. You will land on the Make.com homepage.
You will see a page with a purple background and the headline "Work smarter. Automate faster." This is the correct page. If you see a different page, make sure you typed make.com correctly — not make.co or maker.com.
2
Click "Get started free" in the top right corner
Look at the top right corner of the page. You will see a purple button that says Get started free. Click it once. A signup page will load.
If you already have a Make.com account, click Log in instead — it is right next to the Get started button.
3
Sign up with Google — the fastest option
On the signup page you will see a white button that says Continue with Google with the Google logo next to it. Click it. A popup window opens showing your Google accounts. Click on the Google account you want to use for your automations.
If no popup appears, your browser might be blocking popups. Look for a small icon in the address bar that says "popup blocked" — click it and select "Allow popups from make.com". Then click the Google button again.
4
Complete the onboarding — choose Free plan
Make.com will ask you a few questions. When it asks "What would you like to automate?" select Business processes. When it asks about your role, select anything that fits. When it shows you plan options, look for the Free plan — it says "1,000 operations/month" — and click it. You do NOT need to enter a credit card.
1,000 operations per month is enough for your first clients. One automation that runs 10 times per day uses about 300 operations per month. You can have 3 different automations running before needing to upgrade.
Step 2 — Learn the Make.com Dashboard
After signing in you land on the Make.com dashboard. Before building anything, understand what each section means:
1
The left sidebar — navigation menu
On the left side of the screen you see a vertical menu. These are the main sections:
Scenarios — This is where all your automations live. Every automation you build appears here as a "Scenario". Click this when you want to see, edit or create automations.

Connections — This is where Make.com stores the logins for all connected apps. When you connect Gmail, Google Sheets, or OpenAI, they appear here. If a connection stops working, you fix it here.

Templates — Ready-made automations you can copy and adapt. Useful for inspiration but you will build your own from scratch in this course.

Analytics — Shows how many operations you have used this month and how your automations are performing.
2
The main area — your Scenarios list
The large center area shows all your Scenarios (automations). Right now it is empty because you just created your account. In the top right corner of this area you will see a button that says + Create a new scenario. This is the button you will click to build your first automation.
3
The top bar — account and usage
At the very top of the page you see the Make.com logo on the left. On the right you see your profile picture or initials. Click on your profile picture to access account settings, billing, and to see how many operations you have used this month.
Step 3 — Create Your First Scenario
1
Click "+ Create a new scenario"
In the Scenarios page, click the button + Create a new scenario in the top right corner. The Scenario editor opens — a dark canvas with a grid of dots in the background.
The Scenario editor is where you build automations. Think of it as a blank canvas where you place modules (circles) and connect them with lines. Data flows from left to right through each module.
2
You will see one large circle with a "?" in the center
In the middle of the canvas there is one large circle with a question mark inside it. This is the Trigger — the event that starts your automation. Every Scenario starts with one Trigger. Click on this circle.
A panel slides in from the right side of the screen. At the top of this panel there is a search box that says "Search apps and services". This is where you choose which app triggers your automation.
3
Search for "Google Sheets" and select it
In the search box on the right panel, type Google Sheets. As you type, results appear. You will see "Google Sheets" with a green icon (looks like a grid). Click on it.
After clicking Google Sheets, a list of actions appears. You will see options like "Watch New Rows", "Watch New and Changed Rows", "Create a Row", "Update a Row", etc. For our automation we want to trigger when a new form response comes in.
4
Select "Watch New Rows"
From the list of Google Sheets actions, click on Watch New Rows. This means: "every time a new row appears in this spreadsheet, start the automation." The settings panel for this module opens.
5
Connect your Google Account
The first field in the settings panel says "Connection". Next to it there is a dropdown that says "Select a connection" and a button that says Add. Click the Add button.
A popup window appears with options to connect. Click Sign in with Google.

Your Google account selection popup appears. Click on the Google account that has your spreadsheets.

Google shows you a permission screen saying "Make.com wants to access your Google Account." Scroll down and click Allow.

You are returned to Make.com. The Connection field now shows your Google account name — this means it is connected successfully.
6
Select your Spreadsheet and Sheet
Now configure which spreadsheet to watch:
Spreadsheet field: Click the dropdown. Make.com shows all Google Sheets in your Google Drive. Select the spreadsheet where your form responses go. If you don't have one yet, create a test Google Sheet in Google Drive first.

Sheet field: After selecting the spreadsheet, this dropdown shows the tabs inside it. Select Sheet1 or the tab named "Form Responses 1" if it came from a Google Form.

Limit field: This field says how many rows to process each time the automation runs. Type the number 1 here. This is very important — it prevents the system from processing old rows.
