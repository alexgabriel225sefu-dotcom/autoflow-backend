Tool Setup · Lesson 1

Make.com Setup — Your Automation Hub

Make.com is the platform where you build all your automations visually — no code needed. In this lesson you create your free account and learn every part of the interface so you feel completely confident before building.

What is Make.com and Why We Use It
Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual automation platform. You connect apps together by dragging circles and drawing lines between them. Each circle is an app — Google Sheets, Gmail, OpenAI, WhatsApp — and the lines represent data flowing between them automatically.
Why Make.com and not Zapier? Make.com gives you more flexibility, more control, and 1,000 free operations per month. For your first 2–3 clients the free plan is more than enough. You never need to write a single line of code.
Step 1 — Create Your Free Account
1
Open your browser and go to make.com
Open Google Chrome, Safari, or any browser on your phone or computer. In the address bar at the top, type make.com and press Enter. You will land on the Make.com homepage.
You will see a page with a purple background and the headline "Work smarter. Automate faster." This is the correct page. If you see a different page, make sure you typed make.com correctly — not make.co or maker.com.
2
Click "Get started free" in the top right corner
Look at the top right corner of the page. You will see a purple button that says Get started free. Click it once. A signup page will load.
If you already have a Make.com account, click Log in instead — it is right next to the Get started button.
3
Sign up with Google — the fastest option
On the signup page you will see a white button that says Continue with Google with the Google logo next to it. Click it. A popup window opens showing your Google accounts. Click on the Google account you want to use for your automations.
If no popup appears, your browser might be blocking popups. Look for a small icon in the address bar that says "popup blocked" — click it and select "Allow popups from make.com". Then click the Google button again.
4
Complete the onboarding — choose Free plan
Make.com will ask you a few questions. When it asks "What would you like to automate?" select Business processes. When it asks about your role, select anything that fits. When it shows you plan options, look for the Free plan — it says "1,000 operations/month" — and click it. You do NOT need to enter a credit card.
1,000 operations per month is enough for your first clients. One automation that runs 10 times per day uses about 300 operations per month. You can have 3 different automations running before needing to upgrade.
Step 2 — Learn the Make.com Dashboard
After signing in you land on the Make.com dashboard. Before building anything, understand what each section means:
1
The left sidebar — navigation menu
On the left side of the screen you see a vertical menu. These are the main sections:
Scenarios — This is where all your automations live. Every automation you build appears here as a "Scenario". Click this when you want to see, edit or create automations.

Connections — This is where Make.com stores the logins for all connected apps. When you connect Gmail, Google Sheets, or OpenAI, they appear here. If a connection stops working, you fix it here.

Templates — Ready-made automations you can copy and adapt. Useful for inspiration but you will build your own from scratch in this course.

Analytics — Shows how many operations you have used this month and how your automations are performing.
2
The main area — your Scenarios list
The large center area shows all your Scenarios (automations). Right now it is empty because you just created your account. In the top right corner of this area you will see a button that says + Create a new scenario. This is the button you will click to build your first automation.
3
The top bar — account and usage
At the very top of the page you see the Make.com logo on the left. On the right you see your profile picture or initials. Click on your profile picture to access account settings, billing, and to see how many operations you have used this month.
Step 3 — Create Your First Scenario
1
Click "+ Create a new scenario"
In the Scenarios page, click the button + Create a new scenario in the top right corner. The Scenario editor opens — a dark canvas with a grid of dots in the background.
The Scenario editor is where you build automations. Think of it as a blank canvas where you place modules (circles) and connect them with lines. Data flows from left to right through each module.
2
You will see one large circle with a "?" in the center
In the middle of the canvas there is one large circle with a question mark inside it. This is the Trigger — the event that starts your automation. Every Scenario starts with one Trigger. Click on this circle.
A panel slides in from the right side of the screen. At the top of this panel there is a search box that says "Search apps and services". This is where you choose which app triggers your automation.
3
Search for "Google Sheets" and select it
In the search box on the right panel, type Google Sheets. As you type, results appear. You will see "Google Sheets" with a green icon (looks like a grid). Click on it.
After clicking Google Sheets, a list of actions appears. You will see options like "Watch New Rows", "Watch New and Changed Rows", "Create a Row", "Update a Row", etc. For our automation we want to trigger when a new form response comes in.
4
Select "Watch New Rows"
From the list of Google Sheets actions, click on Watch New Rows. This means: "every time a new row appears in this spreadsheet, start the automation." The settings panel for this module opens.
5
Connect your Google Account
The first field in the settings panel says "Connection". Next to it there is a dropdown that says "Select a connection" and a button that says Add. Click the Add button.
A popup window appears with options to connect. Click Sign in with Google.

Your Google account selection popup appears. Click on the Google account that has your spreadsheets.

Google shows you a permission screen saying "Make.com wants to access your Google Account." Scroll down and click Allow.

You are returned to Make.com. The Connection field now shows your Google account name — this means it is connected successfully.
6
Select your Spreadsheet and Sheet
Now configure which spreadsheet to watch:
Spreadsheet field: Click the dropdown. Make.com shows all Google Sheets in your Google Drive. Select the spreadsheet where your form responses go. If you don't have one yet, create a test Google Sheet in Google Drive first.

Sheet field: After selecting the spreadsheet, this dropdown shows the tabs inside it. Select Sheet1 or the tab named "Form Responses 1" if it came from a Google Form.

Limit field: This field says how many rows to process each time the automation runs. Type the number 1 here. This is very important — it prevents the system from processing old rows.

Click OK when done. The settings panel closes and you see your Google Sheets module on the canvas with a green icon.
✓ Lesson 1 Complete
You now have Make.com set up and your first Trigger configured. The Google Sheets module is on your canvas and connected to your Google account. In Lesson 2 you add OpenAI and Gmail to complete the full automation.
Key Takeaways — Lesson 1
Make.com free plan = 1,000 operations/month. Enough for your first 2–3 clients. No credit card needed.
A Scenario = one complete automation. Trigger + Actions = the full flow.
Always set the Limit field to 1 — this ensures the automation processes one row at a time, not all rows at once.
If a popup is blocked by your browser, look for the blocked popup icon in the address bar and allow it.
✦ Do This Right Now
Go to make.com, create your free account, and add the Google Sheets "Watch New Rows" trigger as described above. Don't move to Lesson 2 until you can see the Google Sheets circle on your canvas with a green icon and your Google account connected. This takes about 10 minutes.
Hands-On Build · Lesson 2

Build a Lead Follow-Up System — Complete Flow

This lesson builds the complete automation: Google Form → Make.com → OpenAI GPT-4o → Gmail. When someone fills out a contact form, they receive a personalized AI-written reply in under 60 seconds. Automatically, forever.

The Complete Flow — What You Are Building
📋
Google Form
Lead fills form
→
▤
Google Sheets
Data saved here
→
◎
OpenAI GPT-4o
Writes the email
→
✉
Gmail
Sends the reply
Step 1 — Create the Google Form
First you need a contact form that captures leads. Here is how to create one in 5 minutes:
1
Go to forms.google.com
Open a new browser tab. In the address bar type forms.google.com and press Enter. Make sure you are logged into the same Google account you connected to Make.com.
2
Click the large "+" to create a blank form
On the Google Forms homepage you see a section called "Start a new form." Click the large colorful + icon that says "Blank" underneath it. A new empty form opens in a new tab.
3
Set the form title
At the top of the form you see "Untitled form" in large text. Click on it and type: Contact Us — or whatever name fits your client's business. Below that there is a "Form description" field — you can leave it empty or type a short description.
4
Add Question 1 — Full Name
The form already has one empty question. Click on "Question" and type Full Name. On the right side of this question box there is a dropdown that says "Multiple choice" — click it and change it to Short answer. Click the toggle that says "Required" to turn it ON (it turns blue).
5
Add Question 2 — Email
Below the first question, click the + button on the right side toolbar to add a new question. Type Email Address. Change the type to Short answer. Turn on Required.
Optional but recommended: Click the 3-dot menu icon (⋮) at the bottom right of this question → click "Response validation" → select "Text" → select "Email address." This prevents people from submitting invalid email addresses.
6
Add Question 3 — Their Problem
Click + again to add a third question. Type What is your main problem or question? Change the type to Paragraph — this lets them write a longer answer. Turn on Required.
7
Link the form to Google Sheets
At the top of the form editor you see tabs: "Questions" and "Responses." Click on Responses. You will see a green Sheets icon (looks like a table with a green background) in the top right of the Responses section. Click it.
A popup appears asking "Select response destination." Click Create a new spreadsheet. The name will auto-fill as "Contact Us (Responses)" — you can keep this name or change it. Click Create.

Google Sheets opens automatically showing your new spreadsheet. The columns are already set up: Timestamp, Full Name, Email Address, What is your main problem or question? This spreadsheet is what Make.com will watch for new rows.
Step 2 — Add the OpenAI Module in Make.com
Go back to Make.com. You should still have the Scenario open with the Google Sheets trigger. Now add the OpenAI module:
1
Hover over the Google Sheets circle to reveal the "+" button
Move your mouse over the right edge of the Google Sheets circle on the canvas. A small circle with a + inside appears. Click it. The module search panel opens on the right.
On mobile: tap and hold the Google Sheets circle, then tap the + button that appears on its right side.
2
Search "OpenAI" and select "Create a Chat Completion"
In the search box type OpenAI. The OpenAI app appears with a black circle icon. Click on it. A list of available actions appears. Find and click Create a Chat Completion.
"Create a Chat Completion" means: send a message to GPT-4o and get a response back. This is the action that generates the personalized email.
3
Connect your OpenAI account — Add API Key
The OpenAI settings panel opens. The first field says "Connection." Click the Add button next to it.
A popup appears with a field called "API Key." You need to get this key from OpenAI. Open a new tab, go to platform.openai.com, create an account if you don't have one, then go to the top right → click your profile → click API Keys → click + Create new secret key → name it "Make.com" → click Create → COPY THE KEY immediately (you cannot see it again) → paste it into the Make.com API Key field → click Save.
4
Select Model: gpt-4o
In the "Model" field, click the dropdown. Scroll until you find gpt-4o and select it. This is the most capable model — it writes the best emails.
5
Set the System message — the AI's personality
Find the field called "System" (sometimes labeled "System message"). Click inside it and type this exact text:
You are a professional and friendly sales assistant. Your job is to write warm, helpful and concise email replies to people who have submitted a contact form. Always be professional, empathetic and end every email with a clear invitation to book a free 15-minute call. Reply in the same language the customer used.
6
Set the User message — with variables from the form
Find the field called "Message content" or "User message." Click inside it. Now you need to mix regular text with variables from your Google Sheets data. Type this:
Write a follow-up email to this person who submitted a contact form: Name: [click variable picker → select Google Sheets module → select "Full Name"] Their message: [click variable picker → select Google Sheets module → select "What is your main problem or question?"] Keep the email under 120 words. Be warm and professional.
How to insert variables: When you see [click variable picker...] in the instructions above, you do NOT type those words. Instead:

1. Position your cursor where you want the variable
2. Click the small icon that looks like a puzzle piece or a variable icon (it is usually to the right of the field or appears when you click inside the field)
3. A list of available modules appears — click on "1. Google Sheets"
4. A list of columns appears — click on "Full Name" for the first variable and "What is your main problem..." for the second
5. The variable appears in the field as a small colored tag like {"1.Full_Name"}
7
Click OK to save the OpenAI module
Scroll to the bottom of the settings panel and click OK. The panel closes. You now have two circles on your canvas: Google Sheets → OpenAI, connected by a line.
Step 3 — Add the Gmail Module
1
Click the "+" on the right side of the OpenAI circle
Hover over the OpenAI circle. Click the + button that appears on its right. The module search panel opens.
2
Search "Gmail" and select "Send an Email"
Type Gmail in the search box. Click Gmail in the results. From the list of actions, click Send an Email.
3
Connect your Gmail account
Click Add next to Connection → click Sign in with Google → select the Gmail account you want to send from → click Allow. The connection is saved.
4
Fill in the "To" field with the lead's email
Click inside the "To" field. Click the variable picker icon. Select 1. Google Sheets → select Email Address. The field now shows {"1.Email_Address"} — this means it will automatically send to whatever email was in the form.
5
Fill in the Subject
Click inside the "Subject" field. Type:
Thank you for reaching out, {"1.Full_Name"}
The {"1.Full_Name"} part is a variable — insert it using the variable picker just like before. The email subject will personalize itself with each person's real name.
6
Fill in the Content with the GPT-4o response
Click inside the "Content" or "Body" field. Click the variable picker. Select 2. OpenAI → then look for choices[] → message → content and click it. The field shows {"2.choices[].message.content"} — this is the AI-generated email text.
7
Click OK to save Gmail settings
Scroll down and click OK. You now have all three modules on your canvas: Google Sheets → OpenAI → Gmail.
Step 4 — Update the Google Sheets Trigger to Your New Sheet
1
Click on the Google Sheets circle to open its settings
Click once on the Google Sheets circle on the canvas. The settings panel opens. In the Spreadsheet dropdown, click it and find the spreadsheet you created from the form — it should be named "Contact Us (Responses)". Select it.
2
Select the correct Sheet tab
In the Sheet dropdown, select Form Responses 1 — this is the tab automatically created by Google Forms. Make sure Limit is still set to 1. Click OK.
Key Takeaways — Lesson 2
The full flow is 3 modules: Google Sheets triggers → OpenAI generates the email → Gmail sends it.
Variables like {"1.Email_Address"} automatically pull real data from the form. Never type email addresses manually.
The Gmail Content field uses {"2.choices[].message.content"} — this is the exact text GPT-4o wrote.
Always connect Gmail with the account you want emails sent FROM — make sure it is your business email.
Test & Launch · Lesson 3

Test Your Automation & Make It Live

Your automation is built. Now you test it with real data, fix any errors, and turn it on permanently. After this lesson your automation works 24/7 without you doing anything.

Step 1 — Submit a Test Form Response
1
Open your Google Form and click the preview button
Go back to forms.google.com. Open the form you created. At the top right of the form editor, click the eye icon (👁) or the "Preview" button. Your form opens as it would look to a real visitor.
2
Fill in the form with your own real information
Fill in the form using your own real name and email address. For the "problem" question, write something realistic like: "I run a small restaurant and I am losing leads because I cannot reply to WhatsApp messages fast enough. I need a solution." Click Submit.
It is important to use your REAL email address for the test — this is where the AI-generated email will be sent so you can check it worked correctly.
3
Verify the response appeared in Google Sheets
Open the Google Sheet linked to your form. You should see a new row at the bottom with your test submission: Timestamp, your name, your email, and your message. If this row is there, the trigger has data to work with.
Step 2 — Run the Test in Make.com
1
Go back to Make.com and open your Scenario
Open Make.com. Go to Scenarios in the left sidebar. Click on the scenario you built. The canvas opens showing your 3 modules.
2
Click "Run once" at the bottom left of the screen
At the very bottom left of the Scenario editor screen, look for a button that says Run once. It has a small play icon (▶). Click it once.
"Run once" means: run the automation one time right now using the newest data in the spreadsheet. It does NOT turn on automatic scheduling — it is just for testing.

After clicking, watch the canvas. The modules will light up one by one as data flows through them:
1. The Google Sheets circle lights up green — it found your test row
2. The OpenAI circle lights up green — it generated the email
3. The Gmail circle lights up green — it sent the email

Each module shows a small number inside a bubble — this is how many records it processed. All three should show "1".
3
What to do if a module shows red instead of green
If any module shows red or an error icon, click on that module. An error message explains what went wrong. Common errors:
Google Sheets error: "No data found" — your test row was already processed. Submit the form again with new data, then run once again.

OpenAI error: "Invalid API key" — your OpenAI key is wrong. Go back to platform.openai.com, create a new key, and update it in the Make.com OpenAI connection settings (go to Connections in the sidebar, find OpenAI, click Edit, paste the new key).

OpenAI error: "Insufficient credits" — add $5 at platform.openai.com → Billing → Add to credit balance.

Gmail error: "Authentication failed" — your Gmail connection expired. Go to Connections in Make.com sidebar, find Gmail, delete it, and reconnect by clicking Add in the Gmail module settings.
4
Check your email inbox
Open the email inbox you used in the test form. Within 30 seconds you should see a new email arrive. The sender is the Gmail account you connected. The subject says "Thank you for reaching out, [Your Name]." The email body is an AI-generated professional reply to the message you wrote in the form.
Read the email carefully. It should sound natural, professional and helpful. It should end with an invitation to book a call. This is exactly what your client's leads will receive every time they fill in the contact form — automatically, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, even when the client is sleeping.
Step 3 — Turn On the Automation Permanently
1
Click the "Scheduling" toggle at the bottom left
At the bottom left of the Scenario editor, next to the "Run once" button, there is a toggle switch that says "Scheduling." Right now it is grey and says OFF. Click on it to turn it ON — it turns green.
A popup may appear asking how often to run the scenario. The default is "Every 15 minutes" — this means Make.com checks the spreadsheet for new rows every 15 minutes and processes any new submissions. Click OK to confirm.
2
Save your Scenario
Click the Save button in the top right corner of the editor (or press Ctrl+S on PC, Cmd+S on Mac). Your automation is now live and saving everything.
3
Verify it is running
Go back to the Scenarios list by clicking Scenarios in the left sidebar. You will see your scenario listed. It should show a green dot or "Active" label — this confirms it is running. You can also see the last time it ran and how many operations it used.

How to Deliver This to a Client
When you sell this to a business, here is exactly what you do:
1
Use THEIR Google account, not yours
When building for a client, connect their Google account to Make.com — not yours. Ask them to create a Make.com account, then add you as a team member so you can build in their workspace. This way the automation belongs to them and continues working even if you stop working together.
2
Customize the prompt for their business
Change the System message in the OpenAI module to match the client's brand voice. For example: "You are the friendly AI assistant of [Restaurant Name]. Always mention our specialty dishes and invite people to visit or call us."
3
Add their form to their website
In Google Forms, click the Send button → click the link icon (🔗) → copy the form URL → give it to the client so they can add it to their website or share it directly on WhatsApp and social media.
Key Takeaways — Lesson 3
Always test with "Run once" before turning Scheduling ON — this prevents accidental emails to real people during testing.
If a module shows red, click on it to read the error message — 90% of errors are connection or API key issues that take 2 minutes to fix.
Scheduling ON = automation runs every 15 minutes checking for new rows. It processes any new form submissions automatically.
When delivering to clients always use their Google account and their Make.com workspace — not yours.
